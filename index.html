360+X: A Panoptic Multi-modal Scene Understanding Dataset

Anonymous Authors
NeurIPS 2023

Abstract

In this work, we present a new data set for comprehensive scene analysis that incorporates multiple types of data. Specifically, we captured real-world video and audio data from a 360 panoramic camera and a stereo camera mounted on a pair of glasses, covering various daily activities. Utilizing this setup, we collected various data modalities including front-view video, panoramic video, egocentric monocular video, egocentric stereo video, normal audio, directional audio, and location (GPS) data. The captured data covers a broad range of scenarios and activities in real life, such as indoor, outdoor, campus, shopping centres, cafes, train stations, etc. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first database that covers as many data modalities and viewpoints as possible, mimicking how daily information is accessed in the real world. The introduction of this novel dataset aims to facilitate the understanding of real-world scenes from varying perspectives. A benchmark analysis is presented over this dataset to evaluate the impact of individual data modality and viewpoint on the performance of models trained on them. This analysis encompasses several visual-audio scene understanding tasks, including video activity classification, temporal activity localisation and self-supervised representation learning. Extensive experimental analysis shows the effectiveness of each data modality/perspective in contributing to panoptic scene understanding. We hope this dataset could encourage the community to explore scene understanding problems from more novel perspectives.
